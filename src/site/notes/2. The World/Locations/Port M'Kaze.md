**Port M'Kaze** is a major trading town in northern Kroonstad. Overlooking the Bay of Pigs, it serves as a major trading centre for all of Kroonstad. A critically strategic city, it is the last bastion of civilisation on the wild continent; all other settlements have either been destroyed, or are overrun by monsters and undead.

## Description
The small port city was located at the Bay of Pigs, at the mouths of the River Shoshen and the River Tiryki. It was built atop four hills: Mount Sibasa, Temple Hill, Throne Hill and Yklwazi Hill. 

Vibrant and bursting with color, buildings in the city are adorned in bright shades of blue, green, orange and pink; baskets and clay urns of varied colors and leafy, flowering vines decorate many of the structures around the city. In front of walls decorated with murals of giant reptiles and mythical heroes, buskers and minstrels in bright clothing covered in feathers and shells perform.

## Government
The city is governed by the Seven Merchant Princes, who are a powerful consortium of wealthy traders. They each hold equal vote in a combined ruling council, which could freely create or change the laws of Port M'Kaze. They would also individually act as judges, interpreting the laws with wisdom and flexibility and meting out punishment independently. Their seat of power was in the palace of Goldenthrone.

Each Merchant Prince holds complete monopoly over a sector of trade; Wakanga controls the sale of magic items while Ekene controls the sale of weapons, rain-catchers and canoes for instance. Purchase of items from anyone other than the correct Merchant Prince was considered trading on the black market, which is dealt with particularly harshly.

Aside from this, day-to-day responsibilities of running the city is delegated to family members and underlings, creating a somewhat loose and inefficient government. 

## Culture
Residents of Port M'Kaze spoke the Chultan, a language characterized by its use of inhaled and exhaled vowels, consonants, clicks and a singsong cadence. They are often dressed in light and colorful clothing, such as long shirts and knee-length kilts, pants, and sarongs that were dyed with bright blues, greens, oranges, reds and yellows. The people of the Port enjoyed loud and wild music, believing in savoring every precious moment of life. The city had a thriving literary and theater scene with many active book clubs. Formulaic fiction was extremely popular, notably mythical romances in which heroes saved their lovers from great dangers or terrible fates.

Popular pastimes included watching and betting on daily fights in the Grand Coliseum or on the survival of the condemned fleeing for their lives within Executioner's Run. Locals and tourist alike also enjoyed attending or participating in the city's famous weekly dinosaur races.

The recreational alcohol of choice was tej, a honeyed liquour that was very sweet. Fish and other seafood were staples, and could be bought in bulk at a fish market in the Market Ward where selection tended to be better in the mornings. Cleanliness was very important to the people of the Port, regardless of social classes. Moreover, thanks to the frequent rains, the city itself is also often quite clean.

## Law and Order
The city was mainly patrolled by guards paid for by the Merchant Princes. In addition to that, certain laws - notably those that involved a threat to the Port's social fabric or to legal economic activity - were enforced by [[2. The World/Organisations/The Ytepka Society\|The Ytepka Society]].

Criminals convicted of a crime were sentenced to Executioner's Run, a beast-filled pit in the Old City where the brutal deaths of the condemned serve as cheap entertainment of the city's populace. However, the Run offers a tantalizing promise: if the condemned escapes the pit alive, they regain their freedom and are exonerated. Crimes as minor as theft or offending a merchant prince can get someone thrown into Executioner's Run.

it is possible to legally kill someone in Port M'Kaze. Writs detailing a specified individual and an approved method of killing, known as sanctions, can be purchased with a minimum of 100 gold pieces. The price was of course subject to additional factors, such as the reason for the sanction, the status of the target, and the ability of the purchaser to pay. These were rare, with only ten sanctions traded in a given year.

## Trade
Abundant wealth passes through Port M'Kaze, and a wide variety of both mundane and exotic goods can be found in the city. The majority of foreign trade takes place in the Grand Souk, while locals made more use of the Red Bazaar. As the sale of most goods was governed by the Merchant Princes' monopolies, foreign traders had to work through the Princes' agents to legally do business in the city. Ships arriving into port are all subject to inspection, and customs agents are empowered to confiscate trade goods.

## Defenses
The city was designed with defense as a priority, due to the many pirates that infest the waters of the Bay of Pigs. Parnak, a massive dragon turtle, has a beneficial relationship with the Ytepka Society who fund and feed it to attack pirates.

Surrounded by a jungle teeming with undead, the city also constructed a high stone wall that encompassed the inner wards of the city. The slums in Malar's Throat are outside of this wall, and remain relatively undefended. Moreover, additional walls within the city were constructed in important or wealthy areas for added security. The walls themselves were massive edificies of fitted stone adorned with colorful geometric patterns and images of beasts and heroes. 

Without a standing army, the city is incapable of claiming more land from the undead infested wilds. The guards paid for by the Merchant Princes man the walls, patrol the streets, watch the markets, but primarily guard their personal interests. These guards do little to defend the people in the slums outside the walls. As such, many residents in Malar's Throat organise their own volunteer Citizens' Brigade, sounding warning horns in the event of an undead attack to get people to flee through the gates to safety.
