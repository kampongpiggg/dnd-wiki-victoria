**The Graying** was a disastrous and global event that occurred in 790 AY. A necromantic curse cause by an unknown entity prevented communicating and raising the dead, even through resurrection spells. In addition, creatures previously raised from death began to wither and die.

##  Description
The main effect of the Graying was that the soul of any creature who died instantly disappeared. The fate of these souls were unknown. It was observed that the disappearance occurred so quickly that even liches were unable to feed their phylacteries with fresh souls before they were siphoned away. 

Spells meant to restore the dead failed completely, with the exception of necromantic spells that produced undead. Any attempt to breathe life into the dead (such as *Revivify*, *Raise Dead*, *Resurrection* and *True Resurrection*) not only did not work, but also released a powerful wave of disenchantment magic that disempowers all magical items and causes them to explode. Magic to communicate with the dead such as *Speak with Dead* also no longer worked.

In addition, creatures previously brought back from the dead began to waste away, rot, and eventually die. This effect could not be healed, slowed, or cured. Travelling to different planes of existence did nothing to halt the wasting effect as well. 

Symptoms of those afflicted with the curse include fever, headaches, painful aching joints, nausea and a general feeling of malaise. Many also began to experience buboes in the groin, neck and armpits, which oozed pus and bled when open. For many, their skin and flesh begin to blacken, wither and die. As they neared the end of their lives, victims often suffer acute fever and vomiting of blood. This fever would get so intense that the flesh would spontaneously combust.

## Cause
Beginning shortly as spring drew to a close, priests and adventurers across the world began to notice the effects of the worldwide curse. Soon, the curse was seen as a plague and became the talk of taverns all across [[2. The World/Nations/The Victorian Empire\|The Victorian Empire]]. While in some cases there was a general panic, in other cases it led to derisive jokes at the expense of the wealthy adventurers and their patrons who could afford resurrection services and were not suffering for it. Those afflicted with the curse flocked to places of healing, but the priests and healers could do nothing to halt the decay.

Within three tendays of the onset of the curse now known as the Graying, under the leadership of Sir Maladark Blade of [[2. The World/Organisations/The Victorian Revolutionary Guard\|The Victorian Revolutionary Guard]], the Eyes of the Emperor deduced that the source of the curse was an ungodly necromantic device known as the Devourer. Despite the preliminary success of tracking the device to the jungle continent of Kroonstad, the Eyes of the Emperor faced new complications. The wilds are filled with carnivorous lizard reptiles and ravenous undead. It was common for seasoned adventurers to have perished in the line of duty, which would make a large majority of them unsuitable for the dangers of the continent. As such, the Eyes of the Emperor began enlisting adventuring parties to embark on expeditions to destroy the device.

The latest of these expeditions is [[1. The Party/0. The Party\|the 52nd Expedition]]. Initially, under the advisement of the [[2. The World/Organisations/Chamber of Lords-Advisory\|Chamber of Lords-Advisory]], this Expedition was to be led by the [[2. The World/People/Senator Arianwen Amadeus\|Senator Arianwen Amadeus]]; however, her daughter Vilde Amadeus stole her imperial summons and usurped the position, wishing to spare her mother the fate of a treacherous jungle.
